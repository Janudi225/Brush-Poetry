What is Realism?

Whether depicting nature or modern life, realism in the arts refers to a precise, thorough, and unadorned rendering. The idealization of the imagination is rejected by realism in favor of a rigorous examination of outer manifestations. As a result, realism has encompassed a variety of artistic currents throughout various cultures.

Painting with realism involves seeing things as they actually are, being able to capture them accurately on canvas or paper, and using real objects and scenes as models.


A bit of history

But, it wasn't until the middle of the 19th century in France that realism was consciouslychosen as an aesthetic agenda. In fact, between 1850 and 1880, realism was a significant tendency in French literature and art. The French realists were in agreement about the importance of contemporaneity in a successful work of art as well as their rejection of the academic schools' artificial Classicism and Romanticism. They made an effort to capture the issues, norms, and social mores of the middle and lower classes as well as the social and physical contexts in which they existed.Many intellectual advancements in the first half of the 19th century encouraged realism. They included the German anti-Romantic movement, which placed a strong focus on the common man as a subject for art.

Characteristics of Realism Art

  • Focuses on representation of the real world.
  • Takes many forms as landscape painting, portraiture or still life.
  • Captures details and edges of objects in order to covey the accurate sense of what they look like.
  • Uses flat colours and shading to capture the light and shadow, texture ans other aspects of the object.
  • Uses plain backgrounds and simple props to minimize unnecessary distractions from the main element.
  • Mostly uses oil paint.
